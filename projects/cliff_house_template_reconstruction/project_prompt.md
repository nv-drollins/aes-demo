## SECTION 2 — SITE AND TERRAIN

What does the landscape look like?

Your answer: A steep coastal bluff dropping approximately five metres across the template terrain toward the west and the ocean, with exposed rock, low native coastal scrub, dry grasses, and minimal formal landscaping.

Which direction is the main view?

Your answer: West toward the ocean. Primary living spaces, bedrooms, offices, balconies, and the pool prioritize this view.

What covers the ground?

Your answer: Natural coastal scrub with rock and dry grasses around the architecture; finished hardscape is limited to the driveway, terraces, stairs, and pool deck.

Describe the building pad.

Your answer: A dark charcoal concrete structural pad tied into the slope, with a visible low plinth and crisp horizontal edges.

Describe the retaining walls.

Your answer: Smooth dark concrete retaining walls concentrated along the west terrace, pool edge, and terrain interfaces; visually continuous with the lower-level plinth.

Describe the patio.

Your answer: A large light-grey concrete terrace west of the house, connected to the main living level and organized around a rectangular infinity pool.

Describe the driveway.

Your answer: A dark exposed-concrete approach from the east leading directly to the large integrated garage.

## SECTION 3 — BUILDING OVERVIEW

Describe the architectural style.

Your answer: Minimalist modernist—flat roofs, cantilevered floors, strong horizontal lines, white ashlar stone, smooth concrete slabs, bronze glazing frames, and no ornament.

How many storeys?

Your answer: Three primary levels. Level 1 floor is approximately Z=0.30→0.50 m, Level 2 floor Z=4.00→4.25 m, Level 3 floor Z=7.75→8.00 m, and the upper roof reaches approximately Z=12.30 m.

Approximate total floor area?

Your answer: Approximately 702 m² gross floor area: Level 1 main 240 m², Level 2 main 174 m², Level 2 guest wing 132 m², and Level 3 studio 156 m². Outdoor terraces, balconies, garage, and pool deck are additional.

What are the defining features?

Your answer: Three stacked and offset horizontal volumes; west-facing full-height glazing; cantilevered terraces and balconies; an integrated 132 m² garage; a separate guest wing; a top-level office and maker studio; deep roof plates; and a west infinity pool with catch channel and retaining structure.

What should be avoided?

Your answer: No pitched roofs, traditional ornament, visually heavy railings, blocked ocean views, decorative symmetry, or unrelated forms that change the final model’s strong horizontal composition.

## SECTION 5 — WINDOWS AND GLAZING

Describe the glazing strategy.

Your answer: Maximum floor-to-ceiling glass on the west facade and major balcony fronts; smaller punched and horizontal windows on north and south; restrained frosted glazing on the east service and entry side.

What kind of glass and frames?

Your answer: Lightly tinted glass with bronze anodized aluminium mullions and approximately 80–100 mm frame and reveal depth. Frosted glass is used where privacy is required.
